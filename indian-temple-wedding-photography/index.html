Indian Temple Wedding Photography

India is a land of many cultures and colours. It's a well known fact that Indian weddings are one of the most interesting weddings in the whole world. Anyone who has ever been to any Indian wedding would tell you about the surreal experience they would've had. Not just the Indian hospitality and food, but just the overall aura in the air when two people are getting married in India is just unmatched. This is probably why Indians have one of the most grand weddings of any culture worldwide.

¬†
While there's a section of society that wants that lavish, big-fat Indian wedding, there are also some brides who like to keep it minimal and classy. For such brides, there's always an option of a minimalistic temple wedding in India. If you're a bride and are looking to get married in a temple, let us share some of our recommendations with you.

¬†

Why get married in a temple?

There are many reasons why couples choose to get married inside a temple. For some, it is a way to connect with their religious roots and to have a traditional ceremony that is steeped in meaning. For others, it is a way to create a more intimate and personal setting for their wedding day. Whatever the reason, getting married inside a temple can be a very special and memorable experience.

One of the main reasons why couples decide to get married inside a temple is because of the religious significance. In many religions, marriage is seen as a sacred union between two people. By getting married inside a temple, couples are able to show their commitment to each other in a very public and symbolic way. Additionally, they may also have access to religious resources and support that can help them through their marriage.

What's the significance of religious ceremony in Hindu weddings?

Wedding is a religious event in India. Even though it's celebrated with great pomp and show and with multiple events across several days, the core of a Hindu wedding ceremony is always religious. The bond of a husband and wife is considered sacred in Hindu culture. And what better place to solemnize your sacred bond than getting married inside a temple?
¬†
Another reason why couples may choose to get married inside a temple is because it can create a more intimate setting for their wedding day. While a traditional wedding ceremony may take place in a large hall or church with hundreds of guests in attendance, a temple wedding can be a much more intimate affair.

For couples who are looking to have a more personal and intimate wedding day, getting married in a temple can be the perfect solution. While temples may not be as large or lavish as some other wedding venues, they can provide a more intimate and spiritual setting for your big day.

If you and your partner are both religious, getting married in a temple can be a very meaningful way to start your life together. Even if you're not religious, a temple can still be a beautiful and unique place to get married.

¬†

Before you decide on a temple as your wedding venue, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, make sure that the temple you choose is willing to host weddings. Some temples only allow religious ceremonies, so it's important to check with the temple beforehand. Also, keep in mind that you may need to make some adjustments to your wedding plans to accommodate the temple's rules and regulations.
Overall, choosing a temple as your wedding venue can be a great option. Just be sure to do your research ahead of time and be prepared to make some adjustments to your plans.
